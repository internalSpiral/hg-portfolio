My First Hackathon Experience

H. Gwyn 6 min read

Stepping into my first hackathon felt like entering a digital battlefield—equal parts adrenaline and anxiety. I didn’t know what to expect, but I left with more than just a finished prototype—I gained confidence, friendships, and a hunger to build more.

Why I Joined

It started with curiosity. I saw a campus post about a 24-hour hackathon with free pizza, mentors, and a prize pool. I thought, “Why not?” Despite being a novice, I wanted to challenge myself outside the classroom.

The Team and the Idea

I joined a team of three other students I barely knew. After an hour of brainstorming, we pitched the idea of a disaster relief coordination app that connects local volunteers with emergency needs in real time.

  • React frontend with a clean, minimal UI
  • Firebase backend for real-time data
  • Google Maps API integration

What Went Right

1. Rapid Prototyping

We built a working MVP within 18 hours. Using tools we already knew helped us stay productive.

2. Team Collaboration

We communicated constantly through Discord and managed tasks with Trello. Surprisingly smooth for first-timers!

3. Mentor Feedback

Midway through, a mentor suggested simplifying our UX, which helped us cut unnecessary features and focus on usability.

What We Struggled With

  • Time pressure—we spent too long debugging a deployment issue.
  • Sleep deprivation—coding at 3 AM is a unique kind of madness.
  • Imposter syndrome—seeing others demoing polished projects almost got to us.

What I Learned

Hackathons aren't just about winning—they’re about learning under pressure. I learned how to manage scope, communicate clearly, and keep pushing even when things break (which they did, often).

Most importantly, I learned to trust myself as a developer. I showed up not knowing what I was capable of. I left proud of what we built together.

Would I Do It Again?

Absolutely. Hackathons are chaotic, fun, and deeply rewarding. If you're on the fence about joining one—jump in. You won’t regret it.
